# Realm Ruckus Core Rules

Applicable Rules Version: V1.3  
Updated: 2026-07-14

## Document Status

This is the English player-facing derivative of `规则/V1.3-核心规则.md`.

If this document conflicts with the Chinese source rules, the Chinese source rules take priority. This document must be updated rather than creating a separate rules system.

## Game Overview

- Players: 2–4
- Play Time: 15–30 minutes
- Starting Hand: 5 cards
- Hand Limit: 7 cards
- Main Deck: Monsters and Relics
- Territory Deck: 12 Territories
- Wild Territories: keep 3 face up whenever possible
- Maximum Defenders per Territory: 3

## Goal

Control all 3 Territories in the same Realm. You win immediately.

The four Realms are:

- The Bonewilds
- The Emberlands
- The Ironfang Range
- The Webwood

Each Realm contains 3 Territories.

## Setup

1. Shuffle all Monster and Relic cards together to form the Main Deck.
2. Deal 5 cards to each player.
3. Shuffle the 12 Territory cards to form the Territory Deck.
4. Reveal 3 Territories and place them face up as the Wild Territories.
5. Choose a starting player at random.
6. Take turns clockwise.

No player controls a Territory at the beginning of the game.

## Key Terms

**Monster:** A Monster card in your hand.

**Defender:** A Monster that has been deployed to a Territory.

**Relic:** A one-use effect card. Relics are not Monsters and cannot become Defenders.

**Origin Territory:** The Territory your attacking Monsters leave when you Raid.

**Target Territory:** The opponent-controlled Territory being Raided.

## Territory Control

A Territory with at least 1 Defender is controlled by that Defender’s owner.

A Territory can hold no more than 3 Defenders.

Different players cannot have Defenders in the same Territory at the same time.

## Territory Reset

When a controlled Territory loses its last Defender:

1. It becomes uncontrolled.
2. Return it to the Territory Deck.
3. Shuffle the Territory Deck.
4. Refill the Wild Territories to 3, if possible.

This can happen after combat or because of a card effect.

## Power, Health and Damage

A Monster’s Stars determine all three values:

- Power equals its Stars.
- Health equals its Stars.
- Damage equals its Stars.

When accumulated damage equals or exceeds a Monster’s Stars, it is Defeated and placed in the discard pile unless its card says otherwise.

After combat, all surviving Monsters fully recover.

Relics have no Stars, Health or Damage.

## Turn Structure

On your turn:

1. **Draw:** Draw 1 card from the Main Deck. If the deck is empty, skip this step.
2. **Action:** Take 1 main action, or pass.
3. **Resolve:** Fully resolve the action, card effects and combat.
4. **Hand Limit:** If you have more than 7 cards, discard down to 7.
5. **Check Victory and Endgame:** Check all immediate win and deck-depletion conditions.

You normally take only 1 main action each turn. Extra actions and responses written on cards do not count against this limit.

## Main Actions

Choose one:

- Deploy
- Raid
- Play a Relic

You cannot take an action that has no legal target or cannot legally resolve, unless a card specifically allows it.

## Deploy

Play 1 Monster from your hand to:

- 1 uncontrolled Wild Territory; or
- 1 Territory you control that has fewer than 3 Defenders.

The Monster becomes a Defender. Resolve its entry effect immediately, if it has one.

When you take a Wild Territory, reveal a replacement Territory immediately, if possible.

A Monster cannot Raid directly from your hand.

## Raid

1. Choose 1 Territory you control as the Origin Territory.
2. Choose 1 Territory controlled by another player as the Target Territory.
3. Choose 1–3 Defenders from the Origin Territory as attackers.
4. Defenders not chosen remain at the Origin Territory.
5. Resolve combat.

## Combat

The attacking player chooses the order of their attackers.

Each attacking Monster may attack once.

For each attack:

1. Choose 1 defending Monster as the target.
2. The attacker deals damage equal to its Stars.
3. The defender simultaneously deals damage equal to its Stars back to the attacker.
4. Defeat any Monster whose accumulated damage equals or exceeds its Stars.

Damage on defending Monsters can accumulate across multiple attacks during the same combat.

### Successful Raid

If no defending Monster remains after the attackers finish:

- All surviving attackers move into the Target Territory.
- The attacking player takes control of it.

If no attacker survives to occupy the Territory, the Territory Resets.

### Failed Raid

If at least 1 defending Monster remains after all attackers have attacked:

- Surviving attackers return to the Origin Territory.

If the Origin Territory has no Defender remaining, it Resets.

After combat, all surviving Monsters fully recover.

## Play a Relic

Play 1 Relic from your hand and resolve its text completely. Then place it in the discard pile unless its effect says otherwise.

A response effect may be used when its card permits and does not count as your main action.

## Card Text Rules

- Follow card text exactly.
- If a card requires a legal target and none exists, the effect does not resolve.
- If a card specifies a number without saying “up to,” choose the full number of legal targets.
- “Return to hand” does not Defeat a Monster.
- When a Defender changes control or location, immediately check whether any Territory has become empty.

## Main Deck Depletion

If the Main Deck is empty, players continue taking turns but skip the Draw step.

When a complete round passes in which:

- no Monster or Relic is played; and
- no Territory changes control,

end the game and compare, in order:

1. Number of Territories controlled.
2. Total Stars of all Defenders.
3. Highest number of Territories controlled in a single Realm.
4. Total number of Defenders.
5. If still tied, the game is a draw.

## Immediate Victory

The moment a player controls all 3 Territories in one Realm, the game ends immediately. Do not finish the turn or round.
