## Welcome to Realm Ruckus

The old kingdom has fallen apart, and every monster crew wants a piece of it.

Recruit strange Monsters, claim wild Territories, launch surprise Raids and use magical Relics to disrupt your rivals.

The first player to control all 3 Territories in the same Realm wins immediately.

## What Is in the Box

Current standard-set structure:

- 40 Main Deck cards
  - 36 Monster cards
  - 4 Relic cards
- 12 Territory cards
- 2 double-sided Quick Guide cards

Packaging totals must be updated only after the final printed component list is approved.

## The Four Realms

### The Bonewilds

A pale wasteland of grave ridges, bone towers and restless blue lights.

### The Emberlands

A blazing region of volcanoes, ash peaks and forge-lit dens.

### The Ironfang Range

A rough mountain frontier filled with beast forts, iron gates and giant stone fangs.

### The Webwood

A moonlit forest of giant trees, tangled webs, glowing fungi and hidden springs.

Each Realm contains exactly 3 Territories.

## Card Types

### Monster Cards

Monster cards are played from your hand. Once placed in a Territory, a Monster becomes a Defender.

Stars determine a Monster's Health and Damage.

### Relic Cards

Relics create one-time effects. They are never Defenders and do not have Stars.

### Territory Cards

Territories are the locations players control. A Territory with at least 1 Defender is controlled by that Defender's owner.

## Setup Example

1. Shuffle all Monster and Relic cards into the Main Deck.
2. Deal 5 cards to each player.
3. Shuffle all 12 Territories into the Territory Deck.
4. Reveal 3 Territories as the Wild Territories.
5. Randomly choose a starting player.

Example:

```text
Wild Territories:
Skullcap Ridge
Cinderpeak
Moonlit Springs
```

No player controls any Territory yet.

## Your Turn

At the start of your turn, draw 1 card.

Then choose one main action:

- Deploy
- Raid
- Play a Relic

You may also pass.

After resolving the action, discard down to 7 cards if necessary.

## Deploy Example

You play `Tigerclaw Enforcer` from your hand to an uncontrolled Wild Territory.

It becomes a Defender and you take control of that Territory.

Immediately reveal another Territory so there are 3 Wild Territories again, if possible.

You may instead Deploy to one of your own Territories, as long as it has fewer than 3 Defenders.

## Raid Example

You control Ironfang Pass with 3 Defenders.

Another player controls Cinderpeak with 2 Defenders.

You choose Ironfang Pass as the Origin Territory and send 2 Defenders to Raid Cinderpeak. The third Defender stays behind.

Each attacking Monster attacks once, in the order you choose.

Damage is dealt simultaneously between the attacker and the chosen defender.

If no defending Monster remains, surviving attackers move into Cinderpeak and you take control of it.

If a defender survives after all attackers have attacked, surviving attackers return to Ironfang Pass.

## Combat Example

A 3-Star attacker targets a 2-Star defender.

- The attacker deals 3 damage.
- The defender simultaneously deals 2 damage.
- The 2-Star defender is Defeated.
- The 3-Star attacker survives with damage during that combat.

At the end of combat, surviving Monsters fully recover.

## Territory Reset Example

You control Moonlit Springs with 1 Defender.

A card effect returns that Defender to your hand.

Moonlit Springs now has no Defender, so it:

1. becomes uncontrolled;
2. returns to the Territory Deck;
3. causes the Territory Deck to be shuffled;
4. may later return as a Wild Territory.

## Relic Timing

Most Relics are played as your main action.

A Relic that cancels another Relic may be played as a response when its text allows. It does not use your main action.

Always follow the card text.

## Calling a Player by Name

Some cards instruct you to call a player by name.

The effect only continues if that player responds to the call.

This is a social interaction rule. The caller should clearly address one player, and the response should be audible to the group.

Do not reinterpret a gesture, laugh or unrelated speech as a response unless the group clearly agrees.

## Card Effect Principles

- Resolve card text exactly.
- A required number means the full number, unless the card says “up to.”
- If there are not enough legal targets, the effect does not resolve.
- Returning a Defender to hand is not the same as Defeating it.
- Whenever a Defender moves, immediately check whether a Territory became empty.

## Winning Example

You control:

- Skullcap Ridge
- Gravewind Hollow
- The Restless Cemetery

All three belong to The Bonewilds.

You win immediately, even if the current action has additional nonessential steps remaining.

## Empty Main Deck

When the Main Deck is empty, players continue taking turns but skip drawing.

If a complete round passes with no Monster or Relic played and no Territory changing control, compare:

1. Territories controlled
2. Total Defender Stars
3. Most Territories in one Realm
4. Number of Defenders
5. Still tied: draw

## First-Game Teaching Order

For a new group, explain only:

1. Win by controlling 3 Territories in one Realm.
2. Draw, then choose Deploy, Raid or Relic.
3. Stars equal Health and Damage.
4. Maximum 3 Defenders per Territory.
5. Empty Territories return to the Territory Deck.

Teach specific card effects as they appear.

## Common Mistakes

- Raiding directly from hand.
- Sending Defenders from more than one Origin Territory.
- Forgetting simultaneous counter-damage.
- Leaving an empty controlled Territory in play.
- Treating returned Monsters as Defeated.
- Choosing fewer targets when the card requires an exact number.
- Forgetting to check immediate victory after control changes.

## Product Tone

Realm Ruckus is competitive but playful. Players may bluff, call names, steal cards and disrupt each other, but the game should remain light and social.
